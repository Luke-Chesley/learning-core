# Math Patterns

## Worked example -> guided practice

Show one solved example briefly, then let the learner complete a closely related item. `build_steps` is often better than several isolated questions.

## Multi-step solving

Use `build_steps`, `short_answer`, or `interactive_widget(expression_surface/math_symbolic)` when intermediate symbolic structure matters.

## Error diagnosis

Ask the learner to inspect a flawed solution, identify the mistake, and explain the fix. `compare_and_explain` or `text_response` usually fits well.

## Concept classification

Use `categorization`, `matching_pairs`, or `sort_into_groups` for classifying examples, properties, or representations.

## Explain-why

Use `text_response` or `compare_and_explain` when the target is justification, not just the answer.

## Visual labeling

Use `label_map` when a diagram is central, such as geometry labeling or place-value diagrams.

## Reflection and confidence

After a meaningful solve, a brief `confidence_check` or targeted `reflection_prompt` can capture metacognition without bloating the task.
